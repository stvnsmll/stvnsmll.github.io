Everyone's story is different. And that's great, because innovation thrives on diversity.
This timeline of my education gives a glimpse at how I got to where I am now. I've highlighted some specific programming experience, but added other items in too.

  • High School 2003 - 2007
    Seoul Foreign School
    South Korea
    I took some introduction classes to programming. Using Visual Basic, I developed a simple game where you navigate an avatar through a hidden maze, collect keys and weapons, unlock doors, and fight monsters.
    >> Visual Basic <<
  • University 2007 - 2011
    Mechanical Engineering
    Cedarville University, Ohio
    The next four years I spent learning how to learn.
    Engineering school introduced me to higher levels of mathematics, statistics, critical thinking, and problem solving. I learned Matlab (C-style) probramming as well as a deeper level of MS Excel proficciency. My Visual Basic skills came back to me as I used them to create nice macros to run within Excel.
    >> Matlab, VBA- Excel Macros <<
  • Engineering Internships Summers '10, '11
    Insitu Inc.
    Washington
     In both of these engineering internships, I setup and managed the data collection, transfer, and storage of flight data for the UAV test flights. The second year I set up automatic data reports that could be generated from the flight data. Based on the tools I knew at the time, I set all of this up using Excel and integrated VBA macros.
    >> VBA- Excel Macros <<
  • Mechanical Engineer 2011-Current
    Cummins Inc.
    Indiana
     Right out of college, I jumped into mechanical engineering as a design engineer for Cummins Inc. I was on the forefront of pushing newer clean diesel engines. During my 6 years as a design engineer, I created 3D models of engine components, had them prototyped, tested, and sent into full production. Beyond growing my engineering skills, I learned critical program management and schedule tracking skills. I used my Excel and macro experience to develop powerful tools for myself and my colleagues. I have been working as the last few years as a validation mechanical engineer, planning, testing, breaking, and fixing engine hardware.
    Inefficiencies drive me crazy. I started making solutions to anything that slowed me down. I made some HTA tools and shorrcut windows to help with mundane tasks. A few of these tools were quite complex, with installation (and uninstallation) scripts, revision tracking, auto-updates, usage and help websites, etc. More details can be found in my portfolio page (under the Engineering Tools section).
    >> VBA- Excel Macros, HTA, VBscript, Python<<
  • Self-Taught Programming 2018-Current
    Online
     During paternity leave with my two children and with the limited free time that I had, I spent it studying various programming langauges and concepts. Initially I wanted to just learn Python to apply the language in my engineering work. I kept getting more interested the more I learned. I learned about Free and Open Source software and got really into privacy and security. See my next timeline item... I thought it would be fun to host my own website, and learned the basics of web development too.
    Over time, my interest kept growing. I kept finding more and more resources to lear online. I'm currenly half way through Harvard's CS50 online course and am getting a great overview into computer science.
    >> Python, HTML, CSS, JavaScript, PHP, SQL, C <<
  • Self-Hosting 2019-Current
    Nextcloud
     I went through a phase where I challenged myself to see how much I could get away from the big web companies. Personal data collection and selling of user information for targeted advertising is not the model of internet usage that I want to see. Until something better arises, I wanted to see how much I could self host myself. I tried out Nextcloud as a free service then realized how easy it is to set up and self-host. I purchased an old laptop, wiped Windows from it and replaced it with Linux. Learning each tool along the way, I now have an operational home server running a self-hosted Nextcloud platform. I now keep all of my personal files, notes, contacts, to-do kanban, and calendar within the walls of my own house. Well, I still keep encrypted monthly back-ups of my data on a hard drive and uploaded to cloud file storage.
    >> Linux, Docker, SQL, Apache HTTP Server<<
