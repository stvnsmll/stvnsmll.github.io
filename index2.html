An unexpected journey??? Yup. After nearly 10 years as a mechanical engineer I realized that my passion is programming. I would make myself tools to use in my engineering work - ranging from simple scripts to more complex programs. Eventually I got to the point where I rolled out some of my tools to other users. With this came installation (and uninstallation) scripts, revision tracking, pushed updates, usage tracking, a website for help and instructions, among other complexities.

I've enjoyed my time as a mechanical engineer, but I found myself always thinking about my tools and wanting to spend my work hours adding new features and bug fixing. Frequently while programming, I may glance at the clock and wonder where the hours went.

While at home on paternity leave with my first child, I used the hours of rocking to teach myself Python basics. When home with my second, I learned web development languages (HTML, CSS, and JavaScript). I've tried to find ways to practice and develop my skills and really had some fun with Advent of Code 2019 at the recommendation of my sister and brother-in-law (both programmers).
Big shoutout to my little sis! Although you could have easily excelled in Law, you quit to pursue programming. You've been an inspiration and motivation for me to follow what I love to do too.


Cruise around the site to see more about my portfolio, education, blog, and hobbies. Any feedback about this site or any of projects is always welcome!
